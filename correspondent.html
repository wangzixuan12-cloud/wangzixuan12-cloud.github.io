第38届学生摄影通讯员 · 38th Batch · Best Group Photo Award · 最佳团体照片奖

Selected as a Lianhe Zaobao student photojournalist in the 38th batch of the correspondent programme — one of Singapore's longest-running student journalism initiatives. The programme provides structured training in news values, visual storytelling, editorial judgement, and caption writing, with a clear emphasis on the distinction between photography as expression and photography as journalism.

The 38th batch concluded with a Best Group Photo Award and participation in ZB Info TV broadcast production. Both outcomes followed from the same underlying shift in approach: understanding that a photograph's value in a news context is determined by what it communicates, not how it looks.

通讯员计划最重要的训练内容，是学会问一个问题：这张照片在讲什么新闻？不是"它好看吗"，而是"它有没有新闻价值"。这个问题改变了我对摄影的整体理解，也影响了此后所有的视觉创作工作。

Journalism as a Discipline
新闻摄影作为一门训练

The Zaobao correspondent programme assigns student photojournalists to cover school and community events with the editorial standards of a working news organisation. The assignment is not to produce attractive images — it is to produce images that carry journalistic value: that document what happened, identify what mattered about what happened, and communicate both clearly to a reader who wasn't present.

Training covered news values, visual storytelling principles, caption writing, editorial selection, and the logistics of assignment-based photojournalism. The practical component involved independent coverage of events with submission to editorial review — a feedback loop that was more rigorous than most school photography instruction.

The programme also included training in ethical considerations specific to journalism photography: the distinction between documentation and intrusion, the responsibilities that come with publishing images of real people in real situations, and the editorial judgements involved in selecting which images represent an event accurately.

这个计划不只是教怎么拍，是教为什么拍。那个"为什么"在新闻摄影里有非常具体的答案，而这个答案和艺术摄影的标准是不一样的。

Best Group Photo — 38th Batch
最佳团体照片奖 · 第38届通讯员颁奖

Awarded Best Group Photo at the 38th Zaobao Correspondents awards ceremony. The category is specific: a group photograph is not simply everyone arranged in a frame. It requires reading the dynamics of a group — their relationships to each other, the occasion's significance, the quality of the collective moment — and timing the shutter to capture the group at a point that communicates something true about them, not just that they were present.

Group photography in a journalistic context is technically and editorially demanding. The composition has to manage multiple subjects simultaneously, the lighting conditions at events are rarely optimal, and the subjects are typically engaged in something other than being photographed. Getting a group photo that works — that has genuine journalistic value and not just formal correctness — requires reading the situation accurately and responding quickly.

The award was the recognition; the more significant outcome was the understanding of what makes group photography work as journalism rather than as portraiture.

团体照难拍，难在要捕捉一群人在某一刻最真实的集体状态，而不是摆出来的样子。这个奖说明我们在那个瞬间判断准确了。

Expanded Roles · 延伸经历

Beyond Still Photography

通讯员经历的延伸：从图片到视频，从记录到组织 · From stills to video, from documenting to organising

🏃
Sports Day Organising Committee
第一届通讯员运动会工委会

Served on the organising committee for the first-ever Correspondents Sports Day — a new event format within the programme, representing an expansion of the batch's involvement beyond photography assignments. Responsibilities included planning, logistics coordination, and execution management for the event itself.

The organising role offered a different perspective on events: rather than covering what was happening as a photojournalist, the task was to design and manage what was happening as an organiser. The two perspectives — the journalist's and the organiser's — are usefully complementary for understanding how public events are constructed.

作为活动组织者参与这次运动会，而不是摄影记录者——换了个视角，理解了同一件事的另一面。

📺
ZB Info TV
ZB Info TV 拍摄经历

Participated in filming for ZB Info TV — transitioning from still photography to broadcast journalism production. The medium shift required adapting from single-frame composition to continuous shot construction, from still-image news values to video narrative structure, and from independent photography to coordinated crew work.

The core editorial skill — identifying the story and building the coverage around it — transfers between media, but the execution differs significantly. The ZB Info TV experience provided direct exposure to broadcast production processes and the specific demands of journalistic video content.

换了媒介，核心逻辑一样：找到故事，然后用这个媒介最合适的方式讲清楚。

📚
Systematic Training Programme
系统培训课程

The correspondent programme's training component covers news values, visual storytelling principles, photographic composition in journalistic contexts, caption writing, editorial selection criteria, and professional conduct on assignment. The curriculum is structured around developing editorial judgement — the ability to decide what is worth photographing, what the best image from a session is, and how to contextualise it accurately.

This training formalised and extended the visual instincts developed through personal photography practice, providing a framework for evaluating images beyond aesthetic preference.

从"多拍"到"有意图地拍"：这个培训体系提供的，是一套评价图片的专业标准，而不只是拍摄技术。

What the Correspondent Programme Changed

通讯员训练之前，我评价一张照片的标准是：它好看吗？技术上有没有问题？训练之后，这些问题仍然重要，但它们不再是第一个问题。第一个问题是：这张照片有没有新闻价值？它在告诉读者什么？这个顺序的改变，影响了我之后所有的视觉作品。

Before the correspondent programme, the primary criteria for evaluating a photograph were aesthetic and technical. After it, those criteria remain relevant, but they are no longer primary. The first question is now: does this image have journalistic value? What does it communicate to someone who wasn't there? That shift in evaluative framework has carried over into every visual project since — including the film work, the immersion documentation, and the CLEP music film. The journalism training changed the standard applied to all the other work.
